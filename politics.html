Politics of India

Explore the democratic framework and governance of India.

Introduction to Indian Politics

India is the world's largest democracy with a federal parliamentary system.

  • Gained independence in 1947.
  • Follows a multi-party system.
  • Guided by the Constitution of India.

Government Structure

India has a three-tier governance system.

  • Legislative - Makes laws (Parliament, State Assemblies).
  • Executive - Implements laws (President, Prime Minister, Cabinet).
  • Judiciary - Interprets laws (Supreme Court, High Courts).

Constitution of India

Adopted on 26th January 1950, the Constitution is the supreme law of India.

  • Longest written constitution in the world.
  • Defines the fundamental rights and duties of citizens.
  • Guides the governance structure and political framework.

Political Parties

India has a diverse political landscape with national and regional parties.

  • Major National Parties: BJP, INC, AAP, CPI(M), etc.
  • Regional Parties: TMC, DMK, SP, Shiv Sena, etc.
  • Election Commission regulates political parties.

Elections in India

India follows a free and fair electoral process.

  • General Elections held every 5 years.
  • State elections determine Chief Ministers.
  • Election Commission ensures transparency.
