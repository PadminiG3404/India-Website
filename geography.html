Geography of India

Explore the diverse landscapes, rivers, climate, and biodiversity of India.

Introduction to Indian Geography

India has a vast and diverse geography, stretching from the Himalayas to the Indian Ocean.

  • Seventh-largest country in the world.
  • Borders with China, Pakistan, Nepal, Bhutan, Bangladesh, and Myanmar.
  • Home to diverse landscapes - mountains, plateaus, deserts, and coasts.

Major Landforms of India

India is home to diverse landforms, each shaping its culture and climate.

  • The Himalayas - The world's highest mountain range.
  • Indo-Gangetic Plains - Fertile lands supporting agriculture.
  • Deccan Plateau - A vast plateau rich in minerals.
  • Thar Desert - A hot desert in Rajasthan.

Rivers & Water Bodies

India has several major rivers that support life and agriculture.

  • Ganga - The holiest river of India.
  • Brahmaputra - Flows through the northeast and Bangladesh.
  • Indus - One of the longest rivers, important in ancient history.
  • Godavari & Krishna - Major rivers of southern India.

Climate & Weather Patterns

India has a diverse climate ranging from arid deserts to tropical monsoons.

  • Himalayan Climate - Cold and snowy.
  • Monsoons - Heavy rains during June-September.
  • Tropical Climate - Found in coastal regions.
  • Desert Climate - Found in Rajasthan.

Biodiversity of India

India is home to rich wildlife and several national parks.

  • Royal Bengal Tiger - National animal of India.
  • Asiatic Elephant - Found in southern forests.
  • Great Indian Bustard - Endangered species.
  • Sundarbans Mangroves - Largest mangrove ecosystem.
