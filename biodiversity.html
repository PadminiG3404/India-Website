Biodiversity of India

Discover the rich wildlife, forests, and ecosystems of India.

Introduction to India's Biodiversity

India is one of the 17 megadiverse countries, home to diverse ecosystems.

  • Hosts about 8% of the world's biodiversity.
  • Has over 90,000 species of animals and 47,000 species of plants.
  • Varied habitats from rainforests to deserts.

Wildlife of India

India is home to some of the most iconic species on the planet.

  • Royal Bengal Tiger - Found in national parks like Sundarbans & Ranthambore.
  • Asiatic Elephant - Roams in southern and northeastern forests.
  • One-Horned Rhinoceros - Found in Kaziranga National Park.
  • Snow Leopard - Inhabits the Himalayan regions.

Forests & Flora

India has a wide variety of forest types, supporting rare plant species.

  • Western Ghats - Home to rainforests with rich plant diversity.
  • Sundarbans - Largest mangrove forest.
  • Deodar & Oak Forests - Found in the Himalayas.

Conservation Efforts

India has made efforts to protect its unique wildlife.

  • Project Tiger - Started in 1973 to save tigers.
  • Project Elephant - Focuses on elephant conservation.
  • Wildlife Protection Act (1972) - A law for the protection of species.

Protected Areas

India has over 100 national parks and 500 wildlife sanctuaries.

  • Kaziranga National Park - Home to the one-horned rhinoceros.
  • Jim Corbett National Park - India's first national park.
  • Sundarbans - UNESCO World Heritage Site.
